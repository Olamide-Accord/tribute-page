Ruth Bader Ginsburg

A woman who spent her life fighting for gender equality and women's right.

A Portrait of Ruth Bader Ginsburg

Here is a timeline of her life:

  • 1933: Born in Brooklyn, New York City.
  • 1954: Graduated from Cornell with a bachelor of arts degree in Government
  • 1954: Married Martin Ginsburg
  • 1956: Enrolls in Harvard Law School
  • 1959: Graduated first of her class from Columbia
  • 1970: Co-founded the Women's Rights Law Reporter, the first law journal in the U.S. to focus exclusively on women's rights
  • 1972: Taught at Columbia Law School, where she became the first tenured woman and co-authored the first law school casebook on sex discrimination.
  • 1993: She took her judicial oath as an Associate Justice of the Supreme Court
  • 2002: Inducted into the National Women's Hall of Fame
  • 2010: Ginsburg became the oldest justice on the court at age 77
  • 2020: Died from complications of pancreatic cancer
